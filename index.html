About Me

In August 2019, I'll be starting my PhD in the School of Computer Science at CMU!

My research interests span the intersections of distributed systems (current flavor of the month is blockchain), multi-party machine learning, and attacks and defenses on multi-party ML. Lately, I am especially interested in privacy and security vulnerabilities in federated learning.

I graduated in 2018 with an MSc in Computer Science at the University of British Columbia, where I was a member of the Networks, Systems and Security (NSS) Lab. I worked under the helpful supervision of Ivan Beschastnikh.

In 2016, I finished my BASc Systems Design Engineering at the University of Waterloo, where I spent several work terms across on a wide variety of projects, from developing bioinformatics research tools to building online recommender systems infrastructure at scale with Linkedin.
